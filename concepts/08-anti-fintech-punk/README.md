# 08 — anti-fintech-punk

## Thesis

CredMesh, in this world, is the working-class ledger that fintechs sneered at — and now it sneers back. The brand is a zine photocopied at Kinko's at 3 a.m.: red-and-black riso print, sharpie-marker headlines, typewriter labels, a logomark that someone sketched on graph paper and refused to clean up. Vandalism as legitimacy. The polish is gone. The numbers are real.

Credit, in this world, *feels* like a tally scratched into a wall — five strokes for every account that paid. Not "wealth management." Not a "platform." Not "the future of finance." A receipt. A ledger. A fact that doesn't need a gradient to be true.

The product is for autonomous AI agents borrowing against verifiable receivables — but the *brand* is for the humans who decide which protocol the machines use, and those humans are sick of the purple-glassmorphism uniform. So this concept assumes the readers have already seen Aave, Drift, Jupiter, and want something that looks like it was printed on a Risograph that ran out of toner halfway through. Trust, here, is earned. Not gilded.

## Design rules

1. **Two inks. Period.** Toner black (`#0A0A08`) on photocopy cream (`#F2EEE5`), with riso red (`#D9281E`) as the only accent. No third color. No gradients. Ever.
2. **Use a chunky display face for headlines** (Bungee Inline / Anton / Permanent Marker). Big, blocky, slightly off-register. Hierarchy via *size*, never via tasteful color shift.
3. **Use a typewriter face for labels** (Special Elite, IBM Plex Mono). Labels are STAMPED. SCREAMED. CAPS WITH PERIODS.LIKE.THIS.
4. **Numbers are rendered honestly, large, and monospaced.** A `$2,140,892.18` TVL is shown to the cent. No K/M abbreviations. The number is the artifact.
5. **Never use a circle as a primary shape.** Circles are corporate. Use rectangles, tally bars, slashes, stamp marks, and torn edges.
6. **Photocopy grit is mandatory.** Every surface gets SVG turbulence noise at low opacity. Every block of text gets one or two slightly-jagged baselines.
7. **Stamps and stencils > icons.** Status reads `RECEIVED`, `VERIFIED`, `CLAIMED`, `IN ARREARS` — overprinted in red as if from a rubber stamp, slightly skewed.
8. **Tally marks are the canonical chart primitive.** Bar charts are tally clusters of five. Utilization is a row of vertical strokes with a diagonal slash for every fifth.
9. **Allow misalignment, but only on the ornamentation.** Headlines and stamps sit a degree or two off-axis. Tables and numbers stay perfectly square — the *facts* are exact.
10. **No marketing voice.** Microcopy reads like a printed receipt or a tour-flyer footnote: `PAY NO MORE THAN`, `RECEIVED IN GOOD ORDER`, `XEROXED. NOT SCANNED.`, `BLOCK 322,841,902.`
