# Inspiration — 08 anti-fintech-punk

References I actually used. Each line cites what I lifted.

1. **Crass — *Stations of the Crass* (1979) record sleeve, designed by Gee Vaucher & Penny Rimbaud.**
   https://collections.vam.ac.uk/item/O198060/stations-of-the-crass-record-sleeve-crass/
   *Took:* the offset-litho black-and-red palette and the principle that a sleeve is a *poster* — the subhead bar `// THE LEDGER FOR MACHINES // PRINTED FOR HUMANS //` is sleeve-copy, not UI copy.

2. **Crass — *Feeding Of The Five Thousand* (1978), the "PAY NO MORE THAN £2.00" stencil border.**
   https://www.ox-fanzine.de/interview/cover-ikonen-crass-the-feeding-of-the-five-thousand-lp-small-wonder-1978-5644
   *Took:* the entire ethical-pricing-as-graphic-element idea. Lifted verbatim as the dashboard's `PAY NO MORE THAN THE TRUTH` price-tag and the logomark's footer banner. This is the single most important reference in the concept.

3. **Raymond Pettibon — Black Flag flyer, Santa Monica Civic, June 1983 (xerox).**
   https://www.sturgisantiques.com/items/original-black-flag-flyer-for-june-11-santa-monica-civic-1983-art-by-raymond-pettibon
   *Took:* the four-bar Black Flag construction is the literal skeleton of the logomark — I added a fifth stroke (a riso-red diagonal slash) so it reads as a working-class ledger tally, not a band homage.

4. **Raymond Pettibon — Black Flag at the Whisky / Black Flag + D.O.A. flyer (1980), 8.5×11 offset xerox.**
   https://www.boo-hooray.com/pages/books/ANT845/raymond-pettibon/black-flag-and-d-o-a
   *Took:* the proportions of the headline-stack zine layout (small typewriter date + huge marker headline + body in narrow columns). Echoed in the masthead grid and the colophon footer.

5. **Adbusters — "First Things Next" feature spread (Tom Brown / Paul Shoebridge / James Labounty).**
   http://www.commarts.com/project/6253/adbusters-spread-first-things-next
   *Took:* the formal grid plus thin rules philosophy — Tom Brown's quote ("a very formal grid and just two typefaces… very thin rules to help structure columns") is exactly the discipline I used to keep the dashboard from collapsing into pure chaos. The numbers stay rectilinear; only the *ornamentation* skews.

6. **Adbusters — "We decided to call our magazine Adbusters" (Kalle Lasn manifesto on layout).**
   https://www.adbusters.org/article/we-decided-to-call-our-magazine-adbusters
   *Took:* the directives "kill the page numbers… abandon the sanitized modernist look… handwriting and doodling all over the spreads… leave post-it notes and coffee spills." The skewed `RECEIVED IN GOOD ORDER` and `VERIFIED — PAYMASTER #07` floaters are the post-it move.

7. **Jonathan Barnbrook — Adbusters art-direction, Occupy London identity (red-and-black typographic mark).**
   http://www.itsnicethat.com/articles/jonathan-barnbrook-adbusters-protest-anonymous-200416
   *Took:* the discipline that a protest mark must be "simple, cheap to reproduce, work in different media — scrawled on a wall or on a large banner." The CredMesh tally logo is designed to be drawable on a wall with a sharpie in five seconds.

8. **VICE Magazine — *Volume 8 Issue 1 / The Design Issue* (2001) and 2001–2002 era spreads (Inkubator).**
   https://www.inkubator.ca/editorialdesign/vice-01-02
   https://www.vice.com/en/topic/volume-8-issue-1
   *Took:* the editorial-voice-in-microcopy move ("Welcome to the VICE School of Design. Thanks again for paying on your way in.") — recast as the dashboard's `XEROXED. NOT SCANNED.` and `NO BAILOUTS` flag-text. Sneer-as-typography.

9. **VICE Magazine — Dave Girard / 3EIGE column on design mistakes ("tits, cars, and arrows").**
   https://www.vice.com/sv/article/design-v10n9/
   *Took:* the prohibition on "the slick tricks" hardened into Rule 1 (two inks only) and the anti-references list. This concept defines itself by what it refuses.

10. **Crass — *Feeding Of The Five Thousand* second-pressing stencil typography ("ANARCHY AND PEACE CRASS").**
    Same Ox-Fanzine source as #2.
    *Took:* the CredMesh wordmark in the masthead is set in Bungee Inline as a stencil-block compromise — Bungee's inline channel reads as "stencil" without being a literal stencil typeface, which would feel costume-y.

## What I deliberately did NOT look at

- Any DeFi dashboard (Aave, Drift, Jupiter, Phantom, Kamino, MarginFi). The whole point of this concept is that *those* are the foil.
- Any "brutalist web design" Awwwards page. That trend is mostly polished-pretending-to-be-brutalist; this concept is the actual artifact.
- Any AI-generated punk-flyer mood board. The references above are first-source.
