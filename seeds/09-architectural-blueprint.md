# Concept 09 — architectural-blueprint

Credit as built system. The brand-world is a drafting room at Le Corbusier's office, 1955. Cyan blueprint paper (background #1B3A6F or so), white drawn lines, hand-drawn line drawings with title blocks, isometric exploded-view diagrams with dimension lines (in mm), Architects Daughter font for handwritten annotations, Trade Gothic Condensed for printed callouts.

The logo is a structural glyph — a column-and-beam joint cross-section, an exploded-view of a girder, a dimensioned bolt detail. The dashboard reads like an architectural section drawing of the credit system, with each subsystem (escrow, reputation, oracle) drawn as a labeled component.

References: Le Corbusier's sectional drawings (Villa Savoye plans), Rem Koolhaas's "S,M,L,XL" book, Bjarke Ingels Group's "Yes is More," Pinterest searches for "structural axonometric," Carlo Scarpa's drafting hand, the Eames House construction drawings.

Credit, in this world, is engineered — bolted together, dimensioned, certified. Every flow has a load path.
