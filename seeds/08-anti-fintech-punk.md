# Concept 08 — anti-fintech-punk

Sneering at DeFi UIs. The brand-world is a zine photocopied at Kinko's at 3am. Photocopy grit (use SVG noise filters), riso-print red+black (or red+blue), sharpie-marker headlines (use a chunky display font like Bungee or Permanent Marker), typewriter ledger labels (Special Elite, IBM Plex Mono Italic), deliberately-jagged-on-purpose lines, spray-paint stencils. ANTI-luxury. Vandalism as legitimacy.

The logo is hand-drawn on graph paper, scanned, never cleaned up. The dashboard refuses to be polished — but it is brutally honest about the numbers.

References: Crass record sleeves (Penny Rimbaud), Adbusters magazine layouts, Suspended publication, early Vice magazine layouts (1996-2002), Oi Polloi zines, the original "Steal This Book" cover, Black Flag flyers by Raymond Pettibon.

Credit, in this world, is the working-class ledger that the fintechs sneered at — and now we sneer back. The numbers are real. The polish is gone. Trust is earned, not gilded.
