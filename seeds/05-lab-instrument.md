# Concept 05 — lab-instrument

Credit as measurement. The brand-world is a Hewlett-Packard test gear catalog from 1973 cross-bred with a JPL flight plan. Everything is calibrated. Oscilloscope traces, micrometer dimension lines, ISO-document layouts, calibration tics. Typography is IBM Plex Mono and IBM Plex Sans — nothing else.

Palette: anodized teal, warm gray, danger red. The logo is a scientific apparatus glyph — a Hall probe, a galvanometer dial, a calibration block, a fiducial mark. The dashboard reads like a measurement instrument's faceplate, with values to four decimal places and units explicit (USDC, BPS, slot).

References: HP 1970s test gear catalogs (the ones with anodized teal panels), NASA Apollo flight plans, Vignelli for Knoll, the cover of Tufte's "Visual Display," Leica's microscope datasheets, Tektronix oscilloscope manuals 1965-1985.

Credit, in this world, is something you measure to four decimal places, with a calibration certificate and a traceable chain of custody back to the standard kilogram.
