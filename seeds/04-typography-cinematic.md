# Concept 04 — typography-cinematic

NO LOGO. Identity is composition + type. Massive Akzidenz-Grotesk Black or Helvetica Now Display 95. Black, white, and ONE saturated accent (consider a single violent red — the kind on a Saul Bass film poster). Brutalist editorial layouts. Every page is a poster. Every header is 240pt. Numbers dominate — share price set in 320pt and centered, credit utilization as a single oversized digit that takes up half the screen.

References: Saul Bass for Hitchcock and Preminger, Massimo Vignelli for Bloomingdale's, Müller-Brockmann's Swiss grids, the cover of Ezra Stoller's photography monographs, Hara Design Institute's MUJI manifestos, Wim Crouwel's New Alphabet experiments.

The logomark IS the wordmark. There is no glyph. There is no icon. There is only typography arranged with such conviction that no glyph is needed.

Credit, in this world, is a cinematic statement — readable from across a trading floor, undeniable.
