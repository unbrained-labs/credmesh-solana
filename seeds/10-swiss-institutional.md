# Concept 10 — swiss-institutional

Credit as Bundesbank-grade canonical issuer. Helvetica Neue 95 Black + 55 Roman + 45 Light. Federal-document layouts. ONE sovereign red accent (use #C8102E or thereabouts). Massive grid (12-column at minimum). ZERO decoration — no rounded corners, no gradients, no shadows, no glassmorphism, no nothing. The page must look like a Bundesbank or ECB statistical bulletin reprinted at a typography conference.

The logo is a wordmark in Helvetica 95, with maybe a single solid mark — a square, a horizontal bar, or no mark at all (the word IS the mark). Numbers are tabular (use Helvetica Neue's tabular figures). Tables dominate.

References: Müller-Brockmann's "Grid Systems in Graphic Design," Swiss bank annual reports (Credit Suisse 1985-1999 era), ECB statistical bulletins, the Helvetica documentary, Knoll's Bauhaus reissue catalogs, Massimo Vignelli's NYC subway map, Karl Gerstner's "Designing Programmes."

Credit, in this world, is a sovereign-grade institutional product, dressed exactly as a central bank would dress it. Quietly serious. No charisma needed.
