# Concept 01 — monastic-ledger

Credit as canonical sacred record. The brand-world is a Cistercian scriptorium reborn: dense Garamond, Adobe Caslon Pro, or EB Garamond on parchment cream, ink-black columns, vermillion ONLY for marginalia and capital initials. Numbers are illuminated. Section headers are Latin. Page composition is dense — small caps, italic captions, leading set tight as a 17th-century printer would.

Anti-fintech to its core: nothing on the page should look like Stripe, Aave, or any Y Combinator startup. References: monastery cartularies, the Domesday Book (1086), the Trinity College Library of Dublin, the Bank of England's manuscript ledgers from 1700-1800, illuminated breviaries.

The logo is a logogram or monogram — possibly a chi-rho-style cipher that encodes "CM", possibly a single illuminated capital. Credit, in this world, is not a financial product — it is the canonical record of agent-to-agent obligation, kept in a ledger as old as commerce itself. Trust is parchment and ink, not a JavaScript bundle.
