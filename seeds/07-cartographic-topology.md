# Concept 07 — cartographic-topology

Credit as trust geography. The brand-world is a USGS 1:24000 quadrangle sheet — but the contours map credit topology rather than terrain. Sepia + cobalt blue. Whitney or Trade Gothic Bold for the labels. Isarithmic data viz — credit flow as contour lines, agent reputation as elevation, defaulted advances as crevasses.

The logo is a hand-engraved cartographic symbol, perhaps a benchmark monument (the brass disc), a triangulation point, or a north-arrow with the CredMesh "C" worked into the rosette.

References: Edward Tufte ("Envisioning Information"), USGS 1:24000 topographic quads, Imhof's relief shading work, NOAA chart 12300 of New York Harbor, Eduard Imhof's "Cartographic Relief Presentation" (1965), Robinson's "Elements of Cartography."

Credit, in this world, has terrain — there are valleys (low utilization), ridges (waterfall priority), unmapped wilderness (unknown agent reputations). The dashboard is a chart you read for navigation.
