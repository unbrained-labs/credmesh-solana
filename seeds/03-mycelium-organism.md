# Concept 03 — mycelium-organism

Credit as living substrate / biological network. The brand-world is a field guide written by a mycologist who is also a software architect. All shapes are organic — root systems, hyphal networks, spore patterns, lichen. NO straight lines except for the gridded margins of the field-guide page itself.

Typography is a serif evocative of natural-history publishing: Lyon Display, Adobe Caslon, or even a botanical-Latin specimen face. Palette: peat brown, chartreuse, ochre, deep moss, parchment cream. The logo is a hand-drawn root cluster or hyphal node with annotation tics, like an Ernst Haeckel plate.

References: Merlin Sheldrake's "Entangled Life," Linnaeus's botanical illustrations, Ernst Haeckel's "Kunstformen der Natur" (1904), the Royal Botanic Gardens Kew herbarium, A.C. Clarke's mushroom journal scans.

Credit, in this world, is mycelium — invisible, distributed, decomposing rent into nutrients. The protocol is the substrate; agents are mushrooms; lenders are spores.
